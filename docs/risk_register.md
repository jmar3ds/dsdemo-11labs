# Fale com a Lu da Magalu: Risk Register

**Companion to:** [`deployment_playbook.md`](deployment_playbook.md), Section 9 (Risk register summary)
**Owner of this register:** ElevenLabs Deployment Strategist, jointly with Magalu CX Ops
**Cadence:** Reviewed in every monthly steering meeting. Mitigation owners report deltas weekly during the 90-day pilot.

This register is the working version of the playbook's risk summary. The playbook lists the headline risks for executive visibility; this document holds the full top 10 with likelihood, impact, owner, and concrete mitigation.

Likelihood and impact are scored Low / Medium / High against the 90-day Phase 1 pilot scope (5% to 15% of opted-in WhatsApp cohort in one region, Luiza premade voice on `eleven_multilingual_v2`, three notification surfaces: abandoned-cart recovery, order status, delivery ETA). A scope change re-opens the register.

Regulatory framing: Magalu retail operations are governed by **CDC primarily, with LGPD for data handling; financial-services scope (BACEN) only when Magalu Pay enters in Phase 4.** Risks tagged "regulatory" reference that framing throughout this register.

## How to read this register

Likelihood and impact are scoped to the Phase 1 pilot. A risk scored Low here may be High at full production scale or in later phases (in particular, the regulatory profile shifts materially when Magalu Pay enters Phase 4), and the register will need a re-score before any expansion past the Day-90 gate.

Two patterns to watch across the register:

1. **Anything scored H impact stays an exec-visible risk even if likelihood drops.** Voice quality drift, latency, PII leakage, hallucination on retail topics, escalation failure, CDC/LGPD exposure, and brand-voice impersonation are the seven we surface in the playbook executive summary for that reason.
2. **Mitigation owners are Magalu-side wherever the risk is about Magalu's environment.** The DS partners on the approach but does not own outcomes the customer must own. This is the deployment-discipline pattern at work.

Note on count: the headline number is "top 10," kept stable for executive reporting. Two retail-specific risks (#11 brand-voice impersonation, #12 marketplace seller voice fraud) are tracked as appended rows because they emerged from the Magalu engagement framing and would not have surfaced on a generic enterprise voice register. They live here for the same reviewer cadence as the top 10.
